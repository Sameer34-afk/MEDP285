History of Chrome

The word "chrome" originates from the Greek word "Chroma", meaning color. This may be how it got its name, but its stylistic properties come from the discovery of Chromium as a mineral. Chromium, discovered in 1797 by Nicolas-Louis Vauquelin, was a metal known for its silver-like appearance. Chromium also became essential in covering objects with a thin layer to give that "Chrome" look.

Within the 1930s, Chrome was used even more, especially within the automotive industry, and design in general. It was used to coat cars and started receiving the reputation as a futuristic material. This eventually evolved into website design, technology, product design, and the idea that chrome represented the future was stapled in society.
